Egusi Soup

Description

Dive into the heartwarming embrace of our Egusi Soup, a Nigerian culinary masterpiece that celebrates rich flavors and nourishing ingredients. Ground melon seeds, or egusi, take center stage in this velvety soup, creating a nutty undertone that complements succulent meats and a symphony of aromatic spices. Served over fluffy rice or pounded yam, each spoonful is a delightful journey through the diverse and comforting taste of Nigerian home cooking. Experience the warmth and depth of Egusi Soup, a dish that embodies both tradition and culinary excellence.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup ground egusi seeds
  • 2 cups assorted meats (beef, goat meat, or tripe)
  • 1 cup spinach or other leafy greens, chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • 2 tablespoons crayfish, ground
  • 2 tablespoons locust beans (iru)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, blended
  • 2 tablespoons ground pepper soup spice
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water or broth

Steps in Preparation

  1. In a pot, heat palm oil and saut√© finely chopped onions until golden.
  2. Add assorted meats, ground crayfish, and locust beans; cook until meats are browned.
  3. Incorporate ground egusi seeds, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  4. Pour in blended scotch bonnet peppers, pepper soup spice, and salt to taste.
  5. Add water or broth, allowing the soup to simmer until the egusi thickens.
  6. Once the meats are tender, add chopped spinach or greens, and let it cook until wilted.
  7. Serve hot and savor the comforting delight of Egusi Soup with your favorite accompaniment.
